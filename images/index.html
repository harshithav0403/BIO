Quality Control Department

Ensuring pharmaceutical excellence through rigorous testing and compliance

Quality Control (QC) plays a vital role in the pharmaceutical industry by ensuring that all products meet the required quality standards before they reach patients.

Role of QC in Pharma

QC ensures:

Compliance with pharmacopeia (IP, BP, EP, USP)
Testing as per SOPs and STPs
Documentation accuracy (ALCOA+ principles)
Instrument calibration & qualification
Proper sample handling and storage
Investigation of OOS, OOT, deviations

GMP Guidelines Related to QC

GMP = Good Manufacturing Practice, focused on ensuring products are consistently produced and controlled.

1

Proper Documentation

All results must be ALCOA+ compliant (Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate). Use of approved formats, worksheets, and notebooks.

2

Qualified Personnel

QC analysts must be trained on instruments, SOPs, and safety.

3

Validated Methods

Testing must follow validated and approved analytical methods (e.g., HPLC, GC, UV).

4

Calibrated Instruments

All instruments must be calibrated and fit for use. Daily checks: balance calibration, pH meter standardization.

5

Handling of Deviations

Investigate and document Out-of-Specification and Out-of-Trend results.

6

Environmental Monitoring

Controlled lab temperature, humidity, and cleanliness.

Raw Materials Department

Ensuring quality from the very beginning with rigorous material testing

Raw material testing ensures that all APIs and excipients meet quality standards before they are used in manufacturing.

QC performs testing as per:

Pharmacopoeia (IP, USP, BP, EP)
In-house specifications
SOP & STP

Types of Tests Done on Raw Materials

Identification Tests

To confirm the material is correct.

Methods: FTIR, UV-Vis, HPLC, GC, Wet chemistry tests

Assay (Purity)

Measures how pure the material is.

Instruments: HPLC (most common), GC, Titration

Related Substances

To detect impurities present in raw materials.

Instruments: HPLC ‚Üí organic impurities, GC ‚Üí volatile impurities

Physical Tests

Melting point, pH, Viscosity, Particle size, Loss on drying (LOD)

Instruments: pH meter, Melting point apparatus, Karl Fischer

In-Process Testing Department

Real-time quality monitoring during pharmaceutical manufacturing

In-process testing (IPT) means checking the quality of a product during manufacturing, not after the batch is finished.

Quality Assurance During Production

It ensures that:

01

The process is going correctly

Continuous monitoring of critical parameters

02

No deviation or error happens

Real-time error detection and prevention

03

The final product will meet specifications

Quality verification at every stage

04

Problems are detected early

Preventing batch failure and ensuring efficiency

Key Aspects of In-Process Testing

Process Monitoring

Continuous monitoring of critical process parameters to ensure they remain within specified limits.

Intermediate Testing

Testing of intermediate products at various stages of manufacturing to verify quality.

Environmental Controls

Monitoring of manufacturing environment including temperature, humidity, and particulate levels.

Equipment Verification

Regular verification that manufacturing equipment is operating correctly and within specifications.

Stability Testing Department

Ensuring product quality and safety throughout shelf life

Stability means how well a drug product maintains its quality over time under different environmental conditions such as temperature, humidity, and light.

Product Quality Maintenance

It checks whether the product remains:

Safe

Free from harmful effects

Effective

Maintains therapeutic effect

Pure

Free from contaminants

Chemically Stable

No chemical degradation

Physically Stable

Appearance, color, pH maintained

Stability studies help determine the shelf life and expiry date of a medicine.
